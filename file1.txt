Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert sunlight into food.
Plants use photosynthesis to produce glucose and oxygen.
Without photosynthesis, life on Earth would not exist.
Photosynthesis occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells.
The rate of photosynthesis depends on light intensity and temperature.
Photosynthesis is one of the most important biological processes on the planet.
Scientists study photosynthesis to develop better solar energy systems.
